Overview

An art tool grounded in aperiodic tilings.

Penrose tilings cover the plane without repeating periodically. That makes them mathematically distinctive and visually rich. Penrose Tiles uses that structure as the basis for composition, making abstract theory accessible through interaction.

The app page is the right place to explain that relationship: what the user can make, what makes the system special, and how the mathematics shapes the visual result.

Core ideas

Aperiodicity

Penrose tilings are famous because they fill the plane without settling into an ordinary repeating grid. That tension between order and non-repetition gives the artwork its character.

On the site, this section can explain why the patterns feel coherent even when they never repeat in the familiar way.

Core ideas

The golden ratio

Many Penrose constructions are linked to the golden ratio through edge lengths, subdivision rules, and the larger proportional structure of the tiling.

This is a strong opportunity for diagrams later if you want to go deeper than prose.
